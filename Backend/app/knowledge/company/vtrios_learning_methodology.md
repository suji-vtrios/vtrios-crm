# VTRIOS LEARNING METHODOLOGY

## Our Training Philosophy

At Vtrios, we believe confidence comes from doing the work yourself.

Many BIM training providers claim to offer "Live Project Training."

In many cases, students are assigned small tasks within an existing project, such as:

- Sheet creation
- Annotation
- Tagging
- Schedule preparation
- Minor model updates

While these activities provide exposure to project environments, they often do not help students develop the confidence to independently deliver a BIM project.

---

# The Vtrios Approach

Instead of assigning students isolated tasks, Vtrios follows a complete project delivery approach.

Every student develops a project from scratch.

Students are guided through the entire lifecycle of a BIM project.

The objective is not simply to learn software commands.

The objective is to understand how a building is developed, documented, coordinated and delivered.

---

# What Students Actually Do

Each student works on their own project.

Students create:

- Project setup
- Levels and grids
- Building modelling
- Walls
- Floors
- Roofs
- Ceilings
- Doors and windows
- Stairs and railings
- Families
- Documentation
- Sheets
- Schedules
- Annotations
- View templates
- Coordination workflows
- Quality checks
- Final deliverables

Students are responsible for developing the project themselves with trainer guidance.

---

# Learning Through Project Ownership

The Vtrios methodology ensures that students:

Do not simply watch.

Do not simply assist.

Do not simply edit someone else's model.

Instead:

They build the project themselves.

This creates a deeper understanding of:

- BIM workflows
- Design intent
- Documentation requirements
- Project standards
- Coordination processes
- Deliverable preparation

Vtrios solves this problem.

By the end of the course, students understand:

- How to develop a model
- How to create documentation
- How to perform quality checks
- How to prepare project deliverables
